Starting point

I had close to zero experience with Angular before the project, making start more troublesome, but after a while started to get hang of it.
Scrum master role was also a first time for me, but had some idea from previous course how scrum works and what is the main point of using scrum in software development environment. Scrum master role brought more things to take into consideration, with new problems needed to get solved.
For testing I had the best starting point, as I like to "break things" and then find out how to fix them. At the start I had high expectations that testing would not be a problem at all during the project.

Planned and outcome

From the start, the design for the website application was already done with little to none changes requires. The only thing that needed real planning was how all the features should be build and what kind of functions and frontend and backend solutions they require to work completely. We planned everything as a group such as; what is the goal of this project, what are appropriate amount of features to call project successful, how project is to be expected to be done, how the product is to be tested during and after developing features, and much more. We had also made many plans on how we hold the meetings, what tools we are expected to use and risk management on what might happen and how to prevent them.

Sadly, these plans did not come to any fruition, and in middle of TiCorporate I left the group, but I did manage to accomplish somethings I had in mind during my short time in the group.
